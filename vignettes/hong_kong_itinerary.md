This bespoke travel itinerary is designed for a seamless journey from Sydney (SYD) to Hong Kong (HKG), balancing iconic city landmarks with the charming, laid-back atmosphere of the outlying islands.

### **Flight Logistics (Qantas)**
*   **Departure:** May 26, 2026 – Qantas Flight QF127 (Sydney to Hong Kong)
*   **Return:** June 1, 2026 – Qantas Flight QF128 (Hong Kong to Sydney)

---

### **Day 1: Arrival & The Harbor Glow (May 27)**
*   **Morning:** Arrive at HKIA. Take the Airport Express to Central. Check into your hotel (recommended: The Upper House or Mandarin Oriental).
*   **Afternoon:** Shake off the jet lag with a stroll through **Hong Kong Park** to see the aviary and the Flagstaff House Museum of Tea Ware.
*   **Evening:** Head to the **Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade** to witness the "Symphony of Lights" at 8:00 PM.
*   **Dinner:** Enjoy your first meal at **The Spaghetti House** (Tsim Sha Tsui branch). It is a nostalgic Hong Kong institution; enjoy their signature Baked Spaghetti Bolognese.

### **Day 2: Peak Views & Colonial Charm (May 28)**
*   **Morning:** Take the historic **Peak Tram** to Victoria Peak. Walk the Lugard Road circular path for breathtaking 360-degree views of the skyline.
*   **Lunch:** *Local Cuisine* – Try a traditional **Dim Sum experience at Luk Yu Tea House** in Central, famous for its 1930s decor and authentic Cantonese flavors.
*   **Afternoon:** Explore the **Man Mo Temple** on Hollywood Road and browse the antique shops in Sheung Wan.
*   **Evening:** Ride the **Central–Mid-Levels Escalator** system and grab a drink at a speakeasy in Soho.

### **Day 3: The Island Escape – Cheung Chau (May 29)**
*   **Morning:** Head to the Central Ferry Piers (Pier 5) and catch the ferry to **Cheung Chau Island**.
*   **Activity:** Rent a bicycle to navigate the island's narrow, car-free streets. Visit the **Pak Tai Temple** and hike the "Mini Great Wall" trail for ocean views.
*   **Lunch:** *Local Cuisine* – Eat like a local by trying **Cheung Chau’s famous "Giant Fishballs"** and fresh seafood at a waterfront restaurant like **Welcome Restaurant**. Don’t miss the Mango Mochi for dessert.
*   **Late Afternoon:** Relax on **Tung Wan Beach** before catching the ferry back to Hong Kong Island.

### **Day 4: Markets & Mong Kok (May 30)**
*   **Morning:** Take the MTR to Kowloon. Wander through the **Yuen Po Street Bird Garden** and the **Flower Market**.
*   **Lunch:** *Local Cuisine* – Visit a **"Cha Chaan Teng"** (Hong Kong-style tea cafe). Order a Pineapple Bun with a thick slice of butter and HK-style Milk Tea.
*   **Afternoon:** Navigate the energetic chaos of the **Ladies' Market** and the **Goldfish Market**. 
*   **Evening:** Enjoy a refined dinner at a high-rise restaurant in Tsim Sha Tsui overlooking the harbor, such as **Hutong**, which offers incredible Northern Chinese cuisine.

### **Day 5: Lantau Island & The Big Buddha (May 31)**
*   **Morning:** Take the MTR to Tung Chung and ride the **Ngong Ping 360 Cable Car** (opt for the crystal cabin). 
*   **Activity:** Visit the **Tian Tan Buddha (Big Buddha)** and the serene Po Lin Monastery. 
*   **Lunch:** Enjoy a vegetarian meal at the monastery's canteen.
*   **Afternoon:** Visit the traditional stilt-house fishing village of **Tai O**. Take a small boat ride to see the pink dolphins if conditions allow.
*   **Evening:** Return to Central for a farewell dinner. Celebrate your final night with a final visit to **The Spaghetti House** if you wish to try their dessert menu or a final classic comfort pasta.

### **Day 6: Last Minute Treasures & Departure (June 1)**
*   **Morning:** Use the morning for final souvenir shopping at the **PMQ (Police Married Quarters)** complex, which showcases local Hong Kong designers and artists.
*   **Lunch:** Grab a quick "Wonton Noodle" lunch at **Mak’s Noodle**—a legendary local establishment.
*   **Afternoon:** Take the Airport Express to HKIA. Ensure you arrive 3 hours early for your Qantas flight home.

---

### **Concierge Tips for Your Trip:**
1.  **Octopus Card:** Purchase an Octopus card immediately upon arrival. It is essential for MTR, ferries (including to Cheung Chau), and even convenience store purchases.
2.  **Footwear:** Bring comfortable walking shoes. Even with the MTR, you will be doing a significant amount of walking.
3.  **Reservations:** For popular dining spots like Luk Yu Tea House or high-end harbor-view restaurants, have your hotel concierge make reservations at least 48 hours in advance. 
4.  **Weather:** May and June are warm and humid with occasional rain showers. Always carry a light umbrella or rain jacket.
