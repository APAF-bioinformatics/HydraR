# Travel Itinerary: Hong Kong Getaway
**Dates:** May 26, 2026 – June 1, 2026  
**Route:** Sydney (SYD) to Hong Kong (HKG) via Qantas  

Welcome to your curated Hong Kong experience. This itinerary balances iconic city skylines with the relaxed, traditional charm of the outer islands.

---

### **Flight Details (Qantas)**
*   **Departure:** May 26, 2026 | QF127 | SYD to HKG
*   **Return:** June 1, 2026 | QF128 | HKG to SYD

---

### **Day 1: Arrival & Harbour Views (May 26)**
*   **Morning:** Arrive at HKG. Take the Airport Express to Hong Kong Station. Check into your hotel (Central or Tsim Sha Tsui recommended).
*   **Afternoon:** Take the **Star Ferry** from Central to Tsim Sha Tsui for the best harbour views.
*   **Evening:** Enjoy a welcome dinner at **The Spaghetti House** (Tsim Sha Tsui branch) to kick off your trip with their signature Italian-fusion comfort food.
*   **Night:** Stroll the Avenue of Stars to see the "A Symphony of Lights" laser show at 8:00 PM.

### **Day 2: The Peak & Colonial Charm (May 27)**
*   **Morning:** Take the historic **Peak Tram** to Victoria Peak. Walk the Lugard Road circular path for panoramic city views.
*   **Lunch:** *Local Cuisine:* Dim Sum at **Luk Yu Tea House** in Central—an institution serving classic Cantonese dishes in a heritage setting.
*   **Afternoon:** Explore the Mid-Levels Escalator and the antique shops on Hollywood Road.
*   **Evening:** Dinner in SoHo. Try **Mak’s Noodle** for their world-famous wonton noodles.

### **Day 3: The Traditional Escape – Cheung Chau Island (May 28)**
*   **Full Day:** Take the ferry from Central Pier 5 to **Cheung Chau Island**.
*   **Activities:** 
    *   Rent a bicycle to tour the island's narrow alleys.
    *   Visit the **Pak Tai Temple**.
    *   Hike to the "Mini Great Wall" for coastal rock formations.
*   **Dining:** Enjoy fresh seafood at one of the open-air restaurants along the **Cheung Chau Praya**. Don’t leave without trying a "Giant Fish Ball" and "Mango Mochi" from the street food stalls.

### **Day 4: Culture & Markets (May 29)**
*   **Morning:** Visit **Man Mo Temple** in Sheung Wan, then wander through the nearby Cat Street market for curios.
*   **Lunch:** Grab a quick "Cha Chaan Teng" meal (local diner style) at **Lan Fong Yuen** for their legendary milk tea and pork chop bun.
*   **Afternoon:** Cross to Kowloon via MTR. Visit the **West Kowloon Cultural District** and the M+ Museum.
*   **Evening:** Explore the Temple Street Night Market. Sample authentic street food like claypot rice and spicy crab.

### **Day 5: Lantau Island & The Giant Buddha (May 30)**
*   **Morning:** Take the **Ngong Ping 360 Cable Car** to Lantau Island.
*   **Activities:** Visit the Tian Tan Buddha (Big Buddha) and Po Lin Monastery.
*   **Afternoon:** Continue to **Tai O Fishing Village** to see the traditional stilt houses. Take a small boat excursion to spot pink dolphins if luck permits.
*   **Evening:** Return to Central for dinner at a high-end Cantonese restaurant, such as **Duddell’s** (Michelin-starred).

### **Day 6: Leisure & Shopping (May 31)**
*   **Morning:** Relaxed breakfast at a local bakery (try a Hong Kong-style egg tart).
*   **Afternoon:** Shopping in Causeway Bay. Visit Times Square and SOGO for some final retail therapy.
*   **Dinner:** A farewell feast at **Ho Lee Fook** for a modern, high-energy take on traditional Chinese cuisine.

### **Day 7: Departure (June 1)**
*   **Morning:** Enjoy a final stroll through **Hong Kong Park** or the Zoological and Botanical Gardens.
*   **Afternoon:** Head to the airport via Airport Express. Use the time to enjoy the Qantas lounge at HKG before your flight home.

---

### **Concierge Tips:**
1.  **Transport:** Purchase an **Octopus Card** upon arrival at the airport. It is essential for MTR, buses, trams, and even the ferry.
2.  **Connectivity:** Pick up a tourist SIM card or ensure your international roaming is active for easy navigation via Google Maps.
3.  **Dining:** *The Spaghetti House* is a local favorite for casual dining—perfect for a reliable, relaxed meal after a busy day of sightseeing.
4.  **Weather:** Late May/Early June is warm and humid. Pack light, breathable clothing and an umbrella for occasional tropical showers.
